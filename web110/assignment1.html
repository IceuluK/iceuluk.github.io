Activity 5: Tracing Internet Traffic
The activity familiarized me with traceroute command and how it helps to identify network problems.
I learned that when sent over Internet, the traffic (packets) goes through a number of routers on the Internet (hops) to reach the desired destination, and traceroute command (in Windows – tracert) helps to trace the paths that traffic (packets) takes.
Traceroute sends Internet Control Message Protocol packets to the destination, with different time-to-live (TTL) values. Each time a packet is handed over to the next router, the TTL is decreased by 1. When TTL reaches 0, the packet is discarded and the router returns “Time Exceeded” message. Traceroute sends the first packet with a TTL of 1 and increases the TTL by 1 on each following packet, until the destination responds or until the maximum TTL is reached. This way, we can see where the traffic has problems on the network (abnormal latencies or stops).
